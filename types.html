Diffuse Nebulae

Diffuse Nebulae are usually categorized into Emission and Reflection Nebulae. Emission Nebula are clouds of ionized gas that emit their own light at optical wavelengths. A Reflection Nebula reflects light from nearby star.

Dark Nebulae

Dark Nebulae form when the interstellar clouds consisting high concentration of dust scatter and absorb all optical light, making them completely opaque or dark. Some of the examples are Horsehead Nebula, Barnard 68, etc.

Supernova Remnant Nebulae

When the core of a massive star reaches a certain threshold, the core collapses sending shock waves outward into the interstellar space, ultimately ending in a supernova explosion. leaving behind a neutron star or black hole. Some examples of Supernova Remnant Nebule are Crab Nebula, Tycho Nebula, Kepler's Nebula and Cassiopeia A.

Planetary Nebulae

Planetary Nebula is kind of an emission nebula formed when a low mass star dies. The glowing shell of ionized gas is ejected into the interstellar space, expanding away from the core. The core eventually becomes the white dwarf. Some examples of Planetary Nebulae are Ring Nebula, Blue Snowball Nebula, Cat's Eye Nebula and Dumbbell Nebula.
